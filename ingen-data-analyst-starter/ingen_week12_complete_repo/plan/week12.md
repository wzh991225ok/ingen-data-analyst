## Headline findings
- **Parts & dispatch = 53% of all process time and 100% waiting.** No amount of headcount shortens it.
- Top 3 stages (Parts & dispatch, Remote diagnosis, On-site repair) = **90%** of all process time.
- The brief's drivers explain only **R²=0.04** of cycle-time variance. Adding one structural fact — did the
  ticket need a part? — takes R² to **0.72** (dispatched tickets +578% [+555%, +601%]).
- **Regional parts buffer: −23%** mean cycle time (P90 64h→47h). **+1 Field FTE: −8%**. **Both: −29%**.
- **Rerouting Tier-1 triage does nothing** (−1.9%, CI spans zero) — triage is 4% of process time.

## Honesty note
Week 7's fact_support_tickets carries no stage/team/workload fields, so the lifecycle layer is GENERATED by a
documented DES model replaying Week 7's real ticket stream. All numbers are properties of that model, **not
measurements of InGen's support org**. Team sizes were chosen to load the teams realistically against the
observed ~6.8 tickets/day, not observed. What transfers is the method + pipeline; the driver regression was
validated against the model's known generating parameters (7/9 effect directions recovered; the 2 misses are
small non-significant product effects diluted below noise). No shift calendar is modelled, so the null weekday
result is not evidence that weekday doesn't matter in reality.
